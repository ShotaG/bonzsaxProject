About Bonz



Hideya "Bonz" Satoh was born in Hokkaido, Japan on November 6th, 1959. He received lessons on the organ from the ages of 4 to 12, and discovered Jazz at the age of 11 while listening to the FM radio.

At the age of 18, he picked up the saxophone without any formal training, and moved to Chiba to attend the Chiba Institute of Technology. By the age of 19, he was playing in Jazz clubs in the Tokyo and Chiba area.

In 1983, upon receiving a scholarship from the Berklee College of Music, Bonz decided to go abroad and study in Boston for a semester. Because his scholarship was extended, he ended up staying until 1986.

At Berklee, he studied under Andy McGee, Jimmy Mosher, Bill Pierce, George Garzone, Herb Pomeroy, And John LaPorta. In addition, he played and jammed with many fine musicians in Boston.

Bonz however, is not EXCLUSIVELY a "jazz musician". His repetoire spans many genres of music, and according to Frrank Wilkins, he was "...one of the most versatile sax players in Boston." After 20 years as a fulltime freelance musician in Boston, Bonz made his move to New York. His New York debut was a gig at the Historical Lennox Lounge Zebra Room.
